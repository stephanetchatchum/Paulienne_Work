01 · What is it?

A savings kit that grows with your dream

KIBO is a physical savings kit inspired by the baobab, Africa's most iconic tree. Its name means “hope” in Swahili.

Every time a child drops a coin into their KIBO jar, a sticker moves one step up their Dream Ladder, turning saving into a visible, exciting journey. When the jar is full, the dream is reached. 🌳

Ages 6–9 No screen needed Made in Africa Parent-friendly

KIBO requires no phone, no app, no electricity: just a jar, a chart, and a dream. Designed for every African child, whether in a city apartment or a rural home.

🫙KIBO Dream Jar (clear)›
A sturdy, transparent jar so children can see their coins piling up day by day. Seeing progress makes saving feel real and exciting. Comes with a secure green lid featuring the KIBO logo.
🪜Dream Ladder Chart (10 steps)›
A colorful wall chart shaped like a growing baobab tree. Each of the 10 rungs represents a savings milestone. The child sticks a gold star on each rung as they reach it, watching Kibo grow before their eyes.
🌟30 gold stickers (milestones)›
Shiny gold star stickers that the child places on the Dream Ladder each time they hit a milestone. 30 stickers support 3 full saving journeys, so KIBO can be used again and again for new dreams.
🃏10 Coin Wisdom Cards›
Small illustrated cards with one simple money lesson each, written in child-friendly language. Examples: “Save 3, spend 1,” “A coin today is a dream tomorrow,” “Ask: do I need it, or do I want it?” One card is earned at each milestone.
🎨My Dream Card (draw it!)›
A blank card the child fills in at the start of each saving journey. They draw or write their dream: a toy, a book, a birthday gift for Mum. The card is pinned at the top of the Dream Ladder as their daily motivation.
🌀Save or Spend? Spinner›
A fun decision-making wheel with everyday scenarios: “A friend offers you candy for 50 francs. Save or spend?” The child spins and discusses the choice with a parent. Builds impulse control and critical thinking through play.

02 · How does it work?

5 simple steps to grow your dream

01
Draw Your Dream
The child draws or writes what they're saving for on their Dream Card: a toy, a book, a gift for Mama. It goes on the wall above the ladder.
02
Drop a Coin
Every coin (whether pocket money, a gift, or earned by doing chores) goes into the clear KIBO jar. Seeing it fill up is magical.
03
Climb the Ladder
At each milestone (every 10 coins or set amount), the child places a gold star sticker on the next rung of their Dream Ladder.
04
Flip a Wisdom Card
Each sticker earns a Coin Wisdom Card with a fun saving tip: “Save 3, spend 1 rule” or “A coin saved is a tree planted.” Short, memorable lessons.
05
Reach the Dream!
When all 10 rungs are filled, the jar is full and the dream is reached. The child opens the jar, counts their coins, and celebrates!

04 · What it teaches

Big lessons for little hands

Saving
Children learn that small, consistent savings add up over time, building the foundational habit of financial independence.
Goal-Setting
Drawing their dream makes it concrete. Tracking progress toward it teaches children how goals are achieved step by step.
Decision-Making
The “Save or Spend?” spinner presents everyday dilemmas (candy now vs. dream later), building healthy impulse control.
Patience
Watching the tree grow slowly teaches children that great things take time, a lesson that extends well beyond money.
Basic Numeracy
Counting coins, tracking milestones, and understanding “how many more?” builds practical math skills naturally.
Cultural Pride
The baobab, a pan-African symbol of strength and community, connects children to their heritage while they learn.
05 · The delight factor

Why a 7-year-old won't want to stop

They can see it happening
The clear jar makes coins visible. Watching it fill up is deeply satisfying; children check it every day. Progress you can see beats progress you're told about.
They own the whole thing
The child names their jar, draws their dream, picks their stickers. KIBO belongs to them, not to parents, not to a bank. This sense of ownership is powerful.
Winning feels real
Gold stickers, Wisdom Cards, and the final tree give children real, tangible rewards at every stage, not abstract points on a screen.
A story they're writing
KIBO turns saving into a narrative: Kibo the baobab grows because of me. That personal agency (“I made this happen”) is the emotion children remember.

“Children don't learn to save by being told to save. They learn by growing something.”

KIBO turns an abstract habit into a physical, joyful act. Every coin dropped is a leaf added to a tree the child planted themselves. That's the DreamDo way: learning through doing.
